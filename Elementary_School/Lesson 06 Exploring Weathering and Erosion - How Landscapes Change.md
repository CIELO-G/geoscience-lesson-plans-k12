# Lesson 6: Exploring Weathering and Erosion - How Landscapes Change

**Grade Level:** 4th and 5th Grade  
**Subject:** Science  
**Duration:** 1 Hour  

---

## OVERVIEW
Students investigate the processes of **weathering** and **erosion**, observing how landscapes evolve over time under the forces of water, wind, and ice. Using 3D printed models, sand trays, and hands-on simulations, they explore how natural forces shape Earth’s surface and reflect on the environmental importance of these processes.

---
![Lesson 01 Figure 01](assets/Lesson06_Figure01.png)

## OBJECTIVES
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Describe the processes of **weathering** and **erosion**.  
- Differentiate between **mechanical** and **chemical** weathering.  
- Demonstrate how **water**, **ice**, and **wind** contribute to erosion.  
- Observe and document how landscapes change under simulated natural forces.  

---

## MATERIALS
- **3D printed landscape models** or plastic terrain trays  
- **Sand, gravel, and small rocks**  
- **Tray or shallow container** (for simulating terrain)  
- **Water** (in spray bottles or cups for rainfall simulation)  
- **Ice cubes** (for freeze–thaw simulation)  
- **Fan or straw** (to simulate wind erosion)  
- **Paper towels** for cleanup  
- **Markers, notebooks, and cameras/tablets** for observations  
- **Visual aids**: diagrams, photos, or short time-lapse videos of landscape change  

---

## PROCEDURE

### 1) Engage (10 minutes)
- Show a short **time-lapse video** of landscapes changing due to weathering and erosion.  
- Ask students:
  - What do you notice about the landscapes?  
  - How do you think these changes happen over time?  
  - Have you seen similar changes around your home or school?  

---

### 2) Explore (25 minutes)
**Hands-On Experiment**
1. Distribute **3D printed models** or **sand trays** with gravel and rocks.  
2. Simulate natural forces:  
   - **Rain** – Spray water over the surface and observe material movement.  
   - **Wind** – Use a **fan** or **straw** to simulate wind erosion.  
   - **Ice** – Place **ice cubes** on the surface to show freeze–thaw effects.  
3. Have students record what changes occur in the terrain after each test.  
4. Discuss:
   - Which materials moved the most?  
   - Which forces caused the greatest changes?  

---

### 3) Explain (10 minutes)
- Define **weathering** (breaking down of rocks) and **erosion** (movement of material).  
- Discuss examples of **water, wind, and ice** erosion in real landscapes.  
- Show diagrams illustrating each process and key vocabulary: *weathering, erosion, sediment, deposition, freeze–thaw, glacier.*  

---

### 4) Evaluate (15 minutes)
- **Mini Quiz:** 3–5 short questions about weathering and erosion.  
- **Reflection:** Students write one paragraph on what they learned and how these processes change Earth’s surface.  
- Optionally, have a few students share their reflections aloud.  

---

## ASSESSMENT
- Observation of participation and collaboration during hands-on activity.  
- Accuracy of quiz and written reflection.  
- Oral discussion demonstrating conceptual understanding.
