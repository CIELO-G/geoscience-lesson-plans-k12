# Lesson 7: Understanding Weather and Climate on a Global Scale

**Grade Level:** 4th–5th Grade  
**Subject:** Science  
**Duration:** 1 Hour 

---

## OVERVIEW
Students investigate the difference between weather and climate by observing local and global weather patterns. They learn how geographic features affect climate and construct their own **wind vanes** using simple classroom materials to record wind direction data. This hands-on approach connects local observations to global phenomena.

---

![Lesson 01 Figure 01](assets/Lesson07_Figure01.png)

## OBJECTIVES
By the end of the lesson, students will be able to:
- Differentiate between **weather** (short-term conditions) and **climate** (long-term patterns).
- Describe how **geographic features** (mountains, oceans, deserts) influence weather.
- Collect and interpret local **weather data** (temperature, wind speed, air pressure, wind direction).
- Construct and use a **wind vane** to measure wind direction.
- Relate local data to global weather trends and patterns.

---

## MATERIALS
- Mini weather station (thermometer, anemometer, barometer)  
- **Wind vane materials:**  
  - Pencil with eraser (pivot base)  
  - Straw (pointer)  
  - Push pin (axle)  
  - Paper (arrow and tail)  
  - Play dough (to hold the pencil upright)  
  - Cardboard (base and N–S–E–W labels)  
- World map or globe  
- Access to online or printed weather data  
- Markers, scissors, tape  
- Reflection sheets or notebooks  

---

## PROCEDURE

### 1) Engage (10 minutes)
Show a brief video/slideshow contrasting global weather (monsoons, blizzards, tropical sunshine).  
Ask:
- What kinds of weather do we experience here?
- How might weather differ in other places?

### 2) Explore (30 minutes)
- Demonstrate the mini weather station instruments and what they measure.  
- Students rotate through stations to take **temperature**, **wind speed**, and **pressure** readings.  
- Provide sample global data and ask them to compare local vs. global patterns.

### 3) Explain (20 minutes)
- Define **weather** vs. **climate**.  
- Explain how **geographic features** (oceans, mountains, deserts) influence weather and climate.  
- Vocabulary: *meteorology, precipitation, humidity, air pressure, wind direction.*

### 4) Elaborate (40 minutes) — Build a Wind Vane
**Assembly**
1. Press a pencil tip-down into a ball of **play dough** so it stands upright.  
2. Push a **pin** through the center of a **straw** and into the pencil’s **eraser** — it should spin freely.  
3. Cut a small **arrowhead** and **tail** from **paper** and tape to the straw ends.  
4. Place the setup on **cardboard** labeled **N, S, E, W** (compass). Align N with true north.  

**Test & Data**
- Take the model outdoors and observe which direction the arrow points (the **direction the wind is coming from**).  
- Record wind direction multiple times over a few days.  
- Compare local wind direction patterns to regional/global weather maps.

### 5) Evaluate (20 minutes)
- Small-group presentations comparing local observations to global patterns.  
- Individual reflections: *“How does geography affect weather where I live?”*

---

## ASSESSMENT
- Group presentation rubric (clarity, accuracy, use of data, visuals).  
- Observation of teamwork, safe tool use, and accurate data recording.  
- Written reflection on understanding of **weather vs. climate** and geographic influences.
