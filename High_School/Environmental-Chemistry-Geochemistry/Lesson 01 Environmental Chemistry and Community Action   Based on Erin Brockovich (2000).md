# Lesson 1: Environmental Chemistry and Community Action  
*Based on* **Erin Brockovich (2000)**

## Grade Level
High School Chemistry (Grades 10–12)

## Duration
1 class period (**90 minutes total**)

## Learning Objectives
Students will be able to:
- Explain the chemistry and toxicology of **hexavalent chromium (Chromium VI)**
- Distinguish between different **oxidation states of chromium** and their health impacts
- Analyze the relationship between **industrial processes** and **environmental contamination**
- Evaluate the role of **citizen science** and **community advocacy** in environmental sustainability
- Apply scientific knowledge to real-world **environmental justice** issues

## Materials Needed
- Clips from *Erin Brockovich* (**approximately 15–20 minutes total**)
  - Scene: Erin discovers the medical records (**19:00**)
  - Scene: Meeting with lawyers (**Scene 9 – 1:21**)
  - Scene: Hinkley visit (**28:08 – 31:20**)
  - Scene: Explanation of chromium contamination (**31:20 – Scene 4 in Apple TV**)
  - Scene: Ending (**02:00:20**)
- Chemistry reference materials on chromium compounds
- Water quality testing information

# Part 1: Introduction and Chemistry Fundamentals (45 minutes)

## Opening Activity (10 minutes)
Show the clip where Erin Brockovich first discovers suspicious medical records attached to real estate files. Ask students:

> **"What would make someone investigate further?"**

**Clip:** Erin discovers the medical records (**19:00**)

## Direct Instruction (20 minutes)

### The Chemistry of Chromium
- Introduce chromium as an element (**atomic number 24**)
- Explain oxidation states, focusing on:
  - **Chromium III (trivalent):** Essential nutrient in trace amounts, relatively stable and less toxic
  - **Chromium VI (hexavalent):** Highly toxic, carcinogenic, strong oxidizing agent
- Chemical form:
  - Cr⁶⁺ typically exists as **chromate (CrO₄²⁻)** or **dichromate (Cr₂O₇²⁻)**
- Industrial uses:
  - Metal plating, corrosion inhibition, cooling towers (**as in the movie**)

### Health and Environmental Impacts
- Chromium VI is a known carcinogen (lung, nasal, sinus cancers)
- Can cause skin ulcers, respiratory problems, kidney and liver damage
- Highly soluble in water, making groundwater contamination dangerous
- Bioaccumulation potential

## Guided Discussion (15 minutes)
Discuss as a class:
- Why would a power company use chromium VI in cooling towers?
- What made this contamination particularly dangerous?
- How does groundwater contamination spread?

# Part 2: Sustainability and Environmental Justice (45 minutes)

## Opening Activity (5 minutes)
Show the clip of the community meeting scene where residents share their health problems.

**Clip:** Hinkley visit (**28:08 – 31:20**)

## Group Activity: Case Study Analysis (25 minutes)
Divide students into groups. Each group examines:

1. **The Science Group**
   - How did chromium VI enter the water supply?
   - What concentrations are dangerous?
   - What are EPA standards?

2. **The Community Impact Group**
   - Who was affected?
   - Why did it take so long to discover?
   - What is environmental justice?

3. **The Corporate Responsibility Group**
   - What are a company's obligations?
   - How do regulations work?
   - What is the precautionary principle?

4. **The Solutions Group**
   - How can such contamination be prevented?
   - What monitoring systems exist?
   - How do we remediate contaminated sites?

## Class Discussion and Presentations (15 minutes)
Each group shares findings. Connect to broader sustainability themes:
- Prevention vs. remediation
- Corporate transparency and accountability
- Community empowerment in environmental protection
- The intersection of science, law, and ethics
